I've sat with this a thousand times in my head — searching for the right words, the perfect sentence, the exact moment — and every time I hesitated, I made you carry a weight you never should have had to carry. That was wrong of me.

There were things I kept from you. Not because they didn't matter, and not because you didn't matter — but because I was afraid. Afraid of being seen completely. Afraid that if you knew every part of me, you might choose to love me less. And so I hid pieces of myself, and in doing so, I cheated you out of the full truth of us.

I am sorry, Esther. Truly. From a place so deep it aches to say out loud.

I'm sorry for the silences that should have been conversations. I'm sorry for the moments I chose comfort over honesty. I'm sorry for not trusting that your love is bigger than my fears — because it is. It always has been.

From this moment forward, I choose you — all the way. I choose transparency, even when it's uncomfortable. I choose to put you first — not second, not almost — first. Before my pride. Before my fear. Before anything else.

You deserve a love that doesn't make you wonder. And I am going to spend every day making sure you never have to wonder again.

You are my home, Esther. And I'm so grateful you're still here.
